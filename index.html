2008 Olympics Data Analysis

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2008 Olympics remains the first memory of Olympic sport for the early
'Gen Z' population. This dashboard visualises the various aspects of
the sport such as the countries with the most number of medals and what
sport they performed the best in.




World map

*Try filtering by continent by clicking on the legend!

As seen from the map, China and the United States have the highest number of Gold medals won at the 2008 Olympics.

Looking at the continents, the Gold Medals seems to be heavily concentrated in Europe. A zoomed in version of Europe is provided below as it can get difficult to tell the countries apart on the world map.

Europe map

Russia , United Kingdom (Great Britain) and Germany have the highest number of Gold Medals in Europe.

There were a total of 24 nations which won a gold medal in Europe. This is by far the highest among the continents with Asia coming in 2nd with 14 nations winnign a Gold medal.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The grouped bar chart below shows the distribution of medals
among the top 10 countries at the Olympic games.

Given information from the previous map, it is not surprising to see that 6 of the 10 positions are taken up by European nations.

The United States seems to have a fairly even split among Gold, Silver and Bronze medals while China's tally is dominated by Gold medals. This anomaly seems to only be prevalent in the Chinese medal distribution as the other countries have a fairly even distribution.

*There are 2 filtering options on this visualisation. Try clicking on the
legend and using the drop down menu below the visualisation.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The heatmap chart shows the distribution of medals won
in each sport by each of the top 10 nations.

Aquatics seems to be the sport that many countries do well in. Ausralia, China , Russia and the United States have won many
medals in Aquatics, which includes all swimming and
synchronised diving events.

The United States and China seem to have their medal tally concentrated in 1 or 2 events whereas the other countries seem to have a much more even spread. The United States won 50%
of its total medal tally across two events; Aquatics and Athletics.
36% of China's medals were won in Aquatics and Gymnastics.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The radial charts below better visualise the distribution
of medals in the United States' and China's 2008 Olympic games campaign.

The United States' seems to be the strongest in Aquatics and
Athletics where it won 33 and 23 medals respectively.
This makes up about 50% of its total medals won at the 2008 Olympic games. The other sports in which the United States' excelled are Cycling, Fencing, Shooting and Gymnastics.

China is also the strongest in Aquatics just like the United States' .
It has equal number of medals in Gymnastics as it did in
Aquatics which means it has a tie for the top spot. These 2 sports make up almost half of its total medal tally. It also fared well in Shooting, Table Tennis, Badminton and weightlifting.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Conclusion

The 2 most successful countries at the 2008 Olympic Games are
China and the United States . China has the most number of Gold Medals
with 51 while the United States has the most number
of overall medals with 111.

All the top 10 nations have at least 1 medal each in Aquatics while
the triathlon was only won by 2 countries due to it being a single event.
Europe was the most successful nation with 24 of its nations winning a gold medal.
